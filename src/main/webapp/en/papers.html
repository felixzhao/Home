The Potential Application of One-dimensional Encoding of Chinese Characters in Statistical Machine Translation

The processing of Chinese characters based on statistical methods has long been troubled by the sparse of the problem space, due to the overwhelming size of character space. The underlying cause of this situation is that single Chinese character has been treated as the smallest unit in common statistical process, which makes methods suitable of alphabetic languages unable to be applied to Chinese corpus successfully. In this study, we introduced one-dimensional encoding of Chinese characters to try to resolve the black box of Chinese. Aiming to minimize and even overcome the wide gap between Chinese language and alphabetic languages, in this study, strived to improve the performance of commonly-used approaches on Chinese corpus and make it possible to deploy more techniques previously applied to alphabetic languages only on Chinese corpus.
